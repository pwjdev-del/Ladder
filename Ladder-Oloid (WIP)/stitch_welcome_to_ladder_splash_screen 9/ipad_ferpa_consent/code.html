Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA) Authorization for Academic Progress & College Advisory Services.

Official Notice of Disclosure

Under the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA), student education records are generally private and may not be disclosed without written consent. By proceeding, you authorize Ladder (the "Service") to share relevant academic information with designated collegiate advisors and institutions.

This authorization remains in effect until the completion of the current application cycle or until revoked in writing. The information disclosed may include transcript data, standardized test scores, behavioral assessments, and extracurricular records necessary for the college preparation process.

Ladder maintains strict adherence to the highest security standards. Your data is encrypted in transit and at rest. We do not sell student data to third-party marketing firms; sharing is limited exclusively to the educational entities you explicitly select within your portal.

I understand that my consent is voluntary and that I may revoke this authorization at any time by contacting the Ladder Compliance Office. Revocation will not affect any disclosures made prior to the receipt of the written revocation notice.

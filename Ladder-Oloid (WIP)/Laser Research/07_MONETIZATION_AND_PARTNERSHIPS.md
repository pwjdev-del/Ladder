## The Mission First

Before monetization: the co-founders were explicit that Ladder should be **free or near-free for the students who need it most** — first-generation, low-income, public school students. Every business model decision should reinforce this:

> "We sell to schools, but we target low-income students. Public schools. Not CSUSA necessarily. Because college prep schools probably have their own thing."

> "If somebody says, I'll give you a scholarship [sponsorship], you have to negotiate: out of 50 students, 40 are free, and then 10 can be from high-income families who pay."

## Revenue Streams

### 1. School/District Subscription (B2B — Primary Revenue)
- **Target:** Public school districts and charter school networks
- **Pitch:** Ladder reduces counselor workload, increases college attendance rates, provides aggregate student analytics
- **Pricing model:**
  - Per-student annual license (e.g., $5–15/student/year)
  - Or flat district fee covering all students in a school
- **Initial target:** Manatee County School District (founders' local district) as pilot
- **Path to scale:** One district approval → use as case study to expand to adjacent Florida counties → national expansion

### 2. Free for Students (First-Gen / Public School)
- Core app features are free for students
- This is not a charity play — it's the product strategy. The district pays on the school side.
- If a student's school is not yet a Ladder partner school, they can still use the app for free with limited features

### 3. Ladder Pro — Premium Student Tier ($9.99/month or $79.99/year)
- Unlimited AI advisor conversations (free tier: 5/day)
- Mock interview sessions
- Full essay review and feedback
- PDF export of application portfolio
- Unlimited college saves (free tier: 3 saves)
- Post-acceptance checklist for unlimited colleges
- **Target:** Higher-income families, students at private/independent schools not covered by district deals

### 4. SAT Tutoring Referrals (Affiliate / Sponsorship)
- **The idea:** Partner with SAT prep services. If they sponsor Ladder, we recommend their service to students at the right time.
- **The right timing:** When app detects student is in 11th grade with SAT registration coming up
- **Example partners:** Khan Academy (free, but can drive engagement), PrepScholar, College Panda, Magoosh
- **Model:** Flat referral fee per student sent, or revenue share on paid courses
- **From transcript:** "Get investors from SAT tutoring sites — they sponsor us, we recommend them to students. Students will likely pay for their services."

### 5. Scholarship Sponsorships
- **The idea:** Scholarship providers (companies, nonprofits, foundations) pay to be featured to relevant students in Ladder's scholarship section
- **How it works:**
  - A company sponsors a $5,000 scholarship for STEM students in Florida
  - Ladder surfaces that scholarship to every 11th grader in Florida with a STEM career path
  - Company pays Ladder a placement/distribution fee
- **From transcript:** "If a scholarship sponsor gives us money, we recommend them. Students who follow the templates can get money for college."

### 6. Government / Education Grants
- **The idea:** Apply for federal and state education technology grants
- **Why it fits:** Ladder's mission (increasing college attendance among first-gen students) aligns directly with Title I funding priorities and state college-readiness initiatives
- **Florida programs:** Florida Lottery-funded scholarships, Bright Futures grant programs
- **Federal:** Department of Education TRIO programs, Gear Up grants
- **From transcript:** "The AI is not free. We need to pay for the APIs somehow. Government funding — we need that."

### 7. College Advertising / Sponsorship (Later Stage)
- Colleges pay to be featured to relevant, matched students
- Example: RIT pays to be surfaced to STEM students with strong extracurricular profiles
- Similar to how Niche.com and College Confidential operate
- **Ethical guardrail:** Never recommend a college based on payment alone — only as a supplement when the college genuinely matches the student's profile

## SAT Fee Waiver (Critical Equity Feature)

Not a revenue stream — but critical to the mission:

- Students who receive free/reduced school meals are automatically eligible for College Board fee waivers
- Waiver covers: 2 free SAT registrations + unlimited free score reports + application fee waivers at many colleges
- **Many eligible students don't know this exists.** Ladder should surface this automatically.
- **Implementation:** During onboarding, ask "Do you receive free or reduced lunch at school?" → if yes, show SAT fee waiver information + step-by-step instructions
- **From transcript:** "I was eligible for free SATs but my counselor took too long to tell me. My parents just paid. A month later, they told me I was eligible."

## Partnership Hierarchy

| Partner Type | Value to Ladder | Value to Partner |
|-------------|----------------|-----------------|
| School districts | Distribution, data, funding | Counselor leverage, analytics |
| SAT prep companies | Revenue, relevance | Access to motivated student audience |
| Scholarship foundations | Revenue | Targeted distribution to eligible students |
| College Board | Legitimacy, data | College prep integration |
| Colleges (later) | Revenue | Targeted recruitment |
| Government (grants) | Funding for free tiers | Education equity outcomes |

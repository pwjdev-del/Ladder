# Activities System — The 4 General + 6 Specific Framework
_Sourced from: subtitles (2).txt, (9).txt, IMG_8237.txt_

---

## Overview

The activities system is **the core of a student's college competitiveness**. Colleges want to see a well-rounded, engaged student — and most students have no idea what that means in practice. Ladder makes it explicit.

There are two layers:

1. **4 General Activities** — Required for ALL students regardless of career path
2. **6 Specific Activities** — Tailored to the student's career path bucket (STEM / Medical / Business / Humanities / Sports)

Each activity is rated on a 1–10 scale by value to college applications.

---

## The 4 General Activities (All Students)

These are the extracurriculars that almost every competitive college expects to see, regardless of major.

### 1. Athletics (or Physical Activity)
- School sports team, club sport, recreational league, or personal athletic pursuit
- **Why:** Colleges value teamwork, commitment, and physical health
- **Leadership bonus:** Captain, team manager, scorekeeper, coach's assistant all count
- **NCAA note:** Student athletes need a separate NCAA compliance track (see `01_MISSING_FEATURES.md`)
- **Suggested rating impact:** Shows commitment + physical balance

### 2. Volunteering / Community Service
- **Florida minimum for Bright Futures scholarship: 120 hours**
- Can be through school clubs, church, community organizations, independent initiatives
- **Leadership bonus:** Organizing/coordinating a volunteer event yourself
- **Ladder tracks:** Hours logged, organization name, dates, description, supervisor contact
- **AI tip when low:** "You're at 30 hours — you need 120 for Bright Futures. Here are 5 volunteer opportunities near your school."

### 3. Clubs (School-Based)
- Minimum: 1 club per year (ideally consistent year over year)
- **Leadership requirement:** Must hold at least one leadership position by senior year
- Types: academic clubs (DECA, FBLA, Key Club, NHS), interest clubs (robotics, drama, debate), identity clubs
- **AI suggestion:** Cross-reference student's career path with relevant club options at their school
- Example: STEM → Robotics Club, Coding Club, Math Team
- Example: Business → DECA, FBLA, Student Government

### 4. Job / Work Experience
- Part-time job, seasonal work, family business
- More valuable than many students realize for showing responsibility, time management
- **Leadership bonus:** Promoted to manager/team lead role
- **AI note:** "Your job at Chick-fil-A shows responsibility and time management — add it to your application activities list"

## The 6 Specific Activities (Career-Path Based)

These are the activities that make a student's application stand out in their chosen field. AI suggests 6 based on the career path, rated 1–10 for college application value.

### Universal Specific Activities (Any Path Can Use)

**Research Paper / Academic Writing (8/10)**
> Every student should write at least one research paper on a topic related to their intended field. It demonstrates intellectual curiosity beyond the classroom.
- Can be submitted to competitions (Regeneron, Google Science Fair, local contests)
- Even a self-published paper on a science blog is more than most students have
- AI prompt: "Pick a topic in [career field] that interests you and research it for 2–3 months"

**Internship or Job Shadowing (9/10)**
> Hands-on experience in the actual field. Hard to get but extremely impressive.
- Even 1–2 weeks of shadowing a professional counts
- AI helps by: suggesting types of internships available for each career bucket + how to find them
- Note from transcript: "It's hard to get an internship, but the suggestion alone helps. Rate it high so students know it's worth pursuing."

**Awards & Competitions (9.5/10)**
> Winning a competition — at any level — is among the highest-impact items on an application.
- National debate tournament win
- Science fair placement (district, regional, state, national)
- Math Olympiad, AMC scores
- Hackathon wins
- Business plan competitions (DECA, FBLA)
- AP score of 5
- SAT 99th percentile in any section
- AI tracks: which competitions apply to student's career bucket + dates

**Informational Interview (7/10)**
> Student interviews a working professional in their target field. Shows active curiosity and initiative.
- Record the interview (with consent) or write a reflection summary
- Can be in-person or Zoom
- AI provides: list of template interview questions for each career path
- Upload: interview summary document or video link to portfolio

### STEM-Specific Activities

**Science Fair / Research Project (8/10)**
- Physics, chemistry, biology, computer science, engineering projects
- Tier: school → district → regional → state → national (Regeneron ISEF)
- AI suggests project ideas based on current curriculum + interests

**Technology Project / App / Website (8.5/10)**
> Building something is proof of ability, not just interest.
- GitHub repo with commits shows consistency
- "I made an app with X users" is extremely impressive
- AI prompt: suggests beginner-friendly project ideas using tools like Replit, GitHub, Unity

**Advanced Math / Statistics (7/10)**
- AP Calculus, AP Statistics, AMC 10/12 participation
- Khan Academy competition certificates
- AI tracks: which AP math courses their school offers + recommends based on GPA

### Medical-Specific Activities

**Hospital / Clinic Volunteering (9/10)**
- Most competitive medical programs expect 100+ hours of clinical experience
- Scribing, hospital volunteer programs, free clinics
- AI helps: list of local hospital volunteer programs by zip code

**CNA / First Aid / CPR Certification (7/10)**
- Shows commitment to the medical path
- Many community colleges offer CNA programs for high schoolers

**Health-Related Research or Paper (8/10)**
- Biology experiment, health study, or written analysis
- Can co-author with a teacher or professor

### Business-Specific Activities

**Entrepreneurship / Side Project (9/10)**
> Starting something — even small — is exceptional.
- Student-run business (Etsy shop, lawn care, tutoring service)
- App with revenue (even $1 in revenue is real entrepreneurship)
- AI prompt: "What's a small business you could start with $0 investment?"

**DECA / FBLA Competition (8.5/10)**
- School chapter participation
- Regional/state/national competition placements
- Leadership positions (chapter officer)

### Humanities-Specific Activities

**Published Writing / Blog / Podcast (8/10)**
- Published article in school paper or local newspaper
- Blog with real readership
- YouTube/podcast with consistent content

**Theater / Film / Art Exhibition (7.5/10)**
- School play participation or lead role
- Short film entered in competition
- Art portfolio for visual arts programs

### Sports-Specific Activities

**Varsity Sports Letter (9/10)**
- Playing varsity at any sport demonstrates elite commitment

**Sports-Related Certification / Coaching (7/10)**
- Referee certification
- Youth coaching / camp counselor
- Athletic training assistant

## How the App Tracks Activities

### Activity Log Card
Each activity logged shows:
- Activity name + category (General or Specific)
- Start date / end date (or "ongoing")
- Hours logged (for volunteering)
- Role / position held
- Leadership position? (yes/no toggle)
- AI-estimated application value (color-coded 1–10 badge)
- Notes field for specifics

### Progress vs. Recommendation
Dashboard widget shows:
- "General Activities: 3/4 completed"
- "Career-Specific Activities: 1/6 started"
- Green: 6+/10 rating items started
- Yellow: important items not yet started
- Red: missing critical items (e.g., 0 volunteer hours by 10th grade)

### Activity Completion vs. Grade Level
AI flags urgency based on grade:
- 9th grade: "Start these soon — no pressure yet"
- 10th grade: "Focus on completing these before SAT prep season"
- 11th grade: "Time is limited — prioritize highest-impact items"
- 12th grade: "Document everything for your Common App activities section"

## Common App Activities Section Integration

The Common App allows 10 activities, 150 characters each for description.
Ladder's activity log is designed to feed directly into this:
- Student taps "Export to Common App" on any activity
- AI generates a 150-character summary of the activity optimized for college applications
- Student reviews, edits, and approves
- Activities ranked by Ladder's impact score (highest first, as recommended by Common App guidance)
